Opportunities for women in technology

  • April 08, 2021
  • ITScholarships

There are a number of events and scholarships that are conducted annually for girls interested in technology. These are great opportunities to learn and network with the community at large. Some of these also provide opportunities to connect with recruiters and gain insight into what work at such organisations is like. Some also provide internship and FTE opportunities. We have summarised the list of most common programs available for women in tech -

1. Generation Google Scholarship (earlier WTM Scholarship)

Established to help aspiring students pursuing computer science degrees excel in technology and become leaders in the field. Selected students will receive $1,000 USD for the 2021-2022 school year.

Selection procedure: Resume, transcript and essay submission for the initial application, followed by an online 30min MCQ test in the next stage. An interview is conducted as the last stage.

Eligibility Criteria: 1st and 2nd year girl students studying Computer Science or a closely related field.

Recipients: Samina Mulani

2. Amazon AmazeWit Program

A diversity hiring initiative by Amazon, a number of online talks and workshop sessions were conducted on how to prepare for coding rounds, interviews etc. A few sessions focused on showcasing Amazon’s work culture were also conducted. This was followed by the assessment procedure.

Selection procedure: An online coding round is conducted post which shortlisted candidates receive an interview invite. There can be multiple interview rounds post which selected students receive an internship offer from Amazon.

Eligibility Criteria: 2nd and 3rd year girls enrolled in a BTech program

Recipients: Anushka Jain

3. Uber She++

A 2-day program held by Uber to promote awareness regarding Uber’s hiring policies and products the company is working on. Selected students get a chance to interview with Uber for a summer internship opportunity.

Selection procedure: Online coding round

Eligibility Criteria: Female students in their 2nd year of engineering

Recipients: Ishita Bhatnagar

4. Twitter DevelopHER Conference

A 2-day event focused on networking and technical insights, aimed at spreading awareness about Twitter’s work culture and diversity initiatives. Participants also got a chance to interact with recruiters. Interview chances were also granted.

Selection procedure: Resume shortlisting was conducted

Eligibility Criteria: Girl students in their pre-final year, studying Computer Science from a recognised university.

Participants: Dipanshi Bansal

5. Goldman Sachs Women Emerging in Finance

A day long onsite event, conducted to provide awareness regarding Goldman Sachs’ diversity hiring policies and an inside look at the career opportunities available at GS in both finance as well as technical fields.

Selection procedure: Had to present a technical solution to some existing societal problem by preparing a document detailing the tech stack involved & impact generated

Eligibility Criteria: 2nd year girls enrolled in a BTech program from selected universities / MBA students in their 1st year from select business schools

Participants: Dipanshi Bansal

6. Adobe Women in Technology Scholarship

An annual diversity hiring initiative conducted by Adobe, this is a prestigious scholarship, only awarded to 5-6 girls all over India. Scholarship is provided, and selected students also get a chance to be an intern at Adobe.

Selection procedure: Essay based questions are to be submitted along with your resume and an LoR from a college professor. Students shortlisted from the first round have to appear for multiple interview rounds before the final shortlist is declared.

Eligibility Criteria: BTech students in their pre-final year

7. GHCI Scholarship

A prestigious scholarship and networking event conducted by the AnitaBorg foundation to encourage and celebrate women in technology.

Selection procedure: 2 essays and a video submission along with LoR from a professor

Eligibility Criteria: Students in their pre-final year

Recipients: Vishnupriya Srivastava

8. JP Morgan Chase Quant Mentorship Program

A year long mentorship program allowing students to interact with JP Morgan’s senior leadership, get mentorship and access to exclusive material regarding mathematical and financial modeling required for Quantitative Finance. Students also get a chance to interview for a Summer Internship opportunity at JPMC.

Selection procedure: An online MCQ round focused on CS and Maths basics, and the second stage being a case study challenge.

Eligibility Criteria: Pre-final year students pursuing a BTech degree

9. DE Shaw DESIS Ascend Program

A 3 day event with various workshops and talks regarding the use of technology in the finance world. Participants got a chance to interact with the leaders of the D.E.Shaw group. All the fellows received goodies and Rs 10000 as part of the fellowship.

Selection procedure: 2 essays to be submitted along with resume

Eligibility Criteria: Pre-final year girl students in a BTech program

Participants: Chahat Jain

10. Microsoft Codess Mentorship Program

An annual mentorship program held virtually - involves 4 assignments and a group project. The pattern of assignments and projects varies every year. There are internship interviews at the end of the program and everyone is given a chance.

Selection procedure: An online MCQ test was conducted post after which people were selected based on their scores

Eligibility Criteria: 2nd year girls students in a BTech program

Participants: Nitya Mangal

11. Adobe SheCodes Program

Countrywide recruitment drive for female interns and full time recruits. Non CS/EEE girls get a shot at applying

Selection procedure: A technical test comprising multiple sections and a typical coding test. Selected students need to appear for an hour long interview which tests DSA primarily, after which the final shortlist is declared.

Eligibility Criteria: Pre-final year girl students from selected universities

Recipients: Smriti Prakash

12. Micron Global Women’s Mentorship Program

Diversity initiative by Micron, a 2 month long program consisting of weekly mentorship sessions, conducted to help girl students get mentored by Micron’s leading women leaders.

Selection procedure: Essay based question and resume to be uploaded

Eligibility Criteria: Pre-final year female students pursuing a graduate or undergraduate degree in Engineering, Computer Science, Math or Physics

13. Nvidia Finding Hopper

Diversity hiring initiative by Nvidia, this program is a recruitment drive aimed at female candidates. Selected students get a summer internship opportunity with Nvidia.

Selection procedure: MCQ round based on Computer Science and Electronics fundamentals consisting of questions based on Digital Design, Computer Architecture and Microprocessors. Also had a coding round with 2 questions to be solved.

Eligibility Criteria: Pre-final year students from selected universities in CS/EE/ECE/EEE branches.

14. Cisco Women in Technology Event

A day long diversity event by Cisco, consisting of interactions with senior leaders at the firm and a Hackathon, top contenders from which will get a chance to interview with Cisco for a summer internship opportunity.

Selection procedure: Shortlisting done based on CGPA and coding round performance

Eligibility Criteria: Pre-final year students from selected universities
